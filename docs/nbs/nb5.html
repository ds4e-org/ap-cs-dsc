General description: The General Social Survey (GSS) is a nationally representative survey of adults in the United States that was started in 1972. Conducted by the non-partisan National Opinion Research Center (NORC), the GSS collects data on a wide variety of topics in contemporary American society, including civil liberties, crime, intergroup tolerance, and social mobility. Its core purpose is to monitor and explain trends in opinions, attitudes, and behaviors.
